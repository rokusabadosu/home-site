Create a client-side web game where the player is a matchmaker in Regency England, aiming to facilitate matches between young gentleman and ladies of the aristocracy over the course of a season, made up of 10 balls.  At the start of each season, dynmically generate 5 gentleman and 5 ladies each with a name and a series of attributes.  Some of these attributes are known to the matchmaker at the start of the season and some can be only be revealed as the player takes certain actions.  The attributes are wealth (known), physical attribute (known), personality (hidden), 3 x interests (hidden), physical attribute that they're attracted to (hidden), personality that they're attracted (hidden).  The characters and their relationships should be represented on a directed graph, with a larger circle representing the character and their name and a series of small emojis showing their attributes or showing where an attribute is hidden.  The player has 10 actions available at start of each ball and uses these actions up to interact with the characters.  The interactions the player can have with the characters are chat (to randomly reveal one of their attributes) introduce (form a relationship between two characters with a relationship score of 1), gossip (reveal a random attribute of someone they have a relationship with), discuss interests (find out who else shares that interest).  When the player has used up all their actions they move on to the next ball.  If you introduce a couple, you "claim" that relationship and you score a point if that couple gets married.
